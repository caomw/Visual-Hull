Multi-view pedestrian tracking

Abstract

Four cameras are mounted on four corners of a room, as Figure 1 shows. The pipeline is:

  1. four cameras capture images simultaneously by boost thread library;

  2. motion detection (GPU MOG2) is utilized to get the binary images of the four views;

  3. Visual-Hull is utilized to get the visual hull of the moving pedestrian;

  4. the ground positions are got by projecting the visual hull into the ground to get the projected image, and then segmenting the projected image by a pre-set threshold, and finally finding centers of the contours, see Figure 2 for an intuitional understanding;

  5. Particle-Filter is utilized to track every individual object, and a process called trajectory management (data association) is utilized to track multi-objects.
